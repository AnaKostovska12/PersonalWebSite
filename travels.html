Greece - everywhere

Greece isn't just a place I visited — it's a destination I return to again and again, each time finding something new to fall in love with. But Greece isn’t only about calm — it’s also about life at its fullest. The vibrant spirit of the Balkans comes alive in unforgettable summer parties, local festivals, and spontaneous dances under the stars that last until dawn. And the food — it’s more than a cuisine, it’s a culture. From perfectly grilled souvlaki to fresh seafood by the water, every meal tells a story of tradition, community, and joy. I’ve found some of my happiest memories gathered around Greek tables, laughing with friends over shared plates and discovering new favorites.

France - Paris

Fun Facts

  • Trust me, The Mona Lisa is definitely smaller than you think.

Visiting France, and Paris in particular, was an unforgettable experience that left me truly speechless. The city’s unparalleled art scene—from the timeless masterpieces in the Louvre to the vibrant street art —immersed me in centuries of creativity and human expression. Standing before iconic works like the Mona Lisa or wandering through the halls of the Musée d’Orsay sparked a deep appreciation for the power of art to capture the human spirit. But beyond the art, Paris was a cultural awakening. The rhythm of everyday life, the conversations over coffee, the effortless blend of tradition and modernity challenged my perspectives and enriched my understanding of the world. It was a place where history and innovation coexist, inspiring me to embrace new ideas and to view life through a broader, more empathetic lens. This journey was more than sightseeing — it was a profound catalyst for personal growth, pushing me to step outside my comfort zone, reflect on my own identity, and cultivate a deeper connection with the diverse cultures that shape our world.

Czech Republic - Prague

Prague holds a special place in my heart — not just for its breathtaking beauty, but for the memories I made there with some of the people I love most. Traveling with friends adds a different kind of magic to any destination, and in Prague, that magic was everywhere — from the cobblestone streets to late-night laughter echoing across historic bridges. The food? Absolutely unforgettable. Traditional Czech cuisine was both comforting and rich — think hearty soups, tender meats, and pastries that feel like hugs from a grandmother . And for someone who loves a good shopping day, Prague is a treasure chest. From charming local boutiques to international fashion houses, the city offered everything — timeless pieces, unique souvenirs, and moments of indulgence. Every street felt like a new opportunity to discover something beautiful. But above all, it’s the memories with my friends that made Prague priceless. Exploring side streets, taking endless photos, getting lost and laughing about it — those are the moments that transformed a trip into something unforgettable.
